How is the President of India elected?

The President of India holds the highest elective office in the country and is elected in accordance with the provisions of the constitution and the Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952. The President holds office for a period of five years from the date on which he enters his office. Currently, President Ram Nath Kovind is serving in the office since July 25, 2017.

Criteria for eligibility of becoming the President of India

1. Citizen of India

2. Above 35 years of age

3. 50 electors to propose; another 50 to second it

4. Should not hold any office of profit

5. Other eligibilities same as an MP

Who elects the President of India ?

The value of votes by all the members of the Electoral College is determined by the provisions of Ariticle 55(2) of the Constitution of India. The value of a vote cast by the MP and MLA is calculated based on the population count of the 1971 census.

The voting process

The voting is based on single transferable vote. The legislators mark their order of preference on the ballot paper and then vote.

The candidate that polls 50% or above in the order of preference wins the round. The least preferred candidate is first eliminated and the votes are distributed to the other candidates based on preference.

The last candidate that makes the 50% cut-off wins and becomes the President of India
